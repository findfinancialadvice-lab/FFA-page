Calculators & Tools

Free tools to help you model your mortgage, tax, KiwiSaver, and more

Numbers make better decisions. The tools below are all free, independent, and built specifically for New Zealand's tax and financial system — covering everything from your take-home pay to your KiwiSaver balance at retirement. None of these calculators require you to create an account or share personal information beyond what you enter.

These calculators are provided by government agencies, regulated financial advice firms, and independent organisations. They are not financial advice — but they are a useful starting point before you speak with a qualified adviser.

Detailed KiwiSaver Calculator

This free KiwiSaver calculator is the most detailed we have ever seen. It is best viewed on a computer as it is very detailed. It can help you understand how different KiwiSaver fund types and contribution levels may affect your long-term retirement outcome, and the time required until a first home purchase is possible. You can test different scenarios, compare contribution rates, and model how changes today could impact your future financial position.

PAYE Calculator

Student Loan Repayment Calculator

Estimate how long your student loan will take to repay based on your current balance and income, and model the impact of making extra voluntary repayments. Accessed via myIR — the tool takes around five minutes and can be a powerful motivator for accelerating repayment.

Inland Revenue (IRD)

Boarder Income Tax Calculator

Work out whether income you receive from boarders or home-stay students is taxable using the IRD's standard costs method. Enter your total gross income from the boarding arrangement, the number of boarders, and their length of stay — the calculator tells you whether you need to include this income in an IR3 return.

Inflation Calculator

The RBNZ's official inflation calculator lets you compare the purchasing power of a dollar amount across any two years in New Zealand's history, drawing on CPI data since 1914. Useful for understanding how the cost of living has changed over time — and what today's prices mean in historical context.
